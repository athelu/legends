## Weapon Properties


**[Light]** Easily wielded in the off-hand. Required for off-hand in Two-Weapon Fighting.

**[Standard]** Default weapons requiring one hand. Cannot be wielded two-handed for benefit.

**[Versatile]** Can be wielded one-handed or two-handed for +1 damage when two-handed.

**[Two-Handed]** Requires both hands to wield. Cannot be used with shields or in Two-Weapon Fighting.

**[Finesse]** Can use either Strength or Agility for damage calculations (player's choice).

**[Alternate-Strike]** Can use secondary attack for different damage type at reduced damage.

**[Multi-Type]** Deals full damage with multiple damage types - player chooses which type when attacking.

**[Ranged]** For ranged attacks using Ranged Combat skill. Has range increments.

**[Thrown]** Can be thrown using Ranged Combat skill, retains damage type and value.

**[Reach]** add +5 feet to melee range.

### Weapon Damage Keywords

**[Bludgeoning]** Blunt damage, relying on crushing, smashing, and often weight to deal damage.
**[Slashing]** Cutting, hacking, slicing, where the sharp edge is drawn across the target.
**[Piercing]** Power concentrated at the tip of the weapon, with thrusts or propulsion.

### Range Format
Ranges listed as Short/Medium/Long (e.g., 60/120/240 means short range 60ft, medium 120ft, long 240ft)

## Weapon Descriptions

### Battleaxe
**Cost:** 40 gp | **Weight:** 6 lbs | **Damage:** 8 | **Properties:** [Versatile]
**Damage Type:** [Slashing]

A single-bladed axe designed for war, with a sturdy haft and a heavy, curved blade. Can be wielded in one hand for versatile combat or two-handed for devastating strikes. This category includes bearded axes, Danish axes, and similar military-grade single-bladed axes. The balanced design makes it equally effective mounted or on foot.

### Buckler
**Cost:** 10 gp | **Weight:** 3 lbs | **Damage:** 4 | **Properties:** [Light], [Standard], [Finesse]
**Damage Type:** [Bludgeoning]

A small, round shield typically 12-18 inches in diameter, used both defensively and offensively. When used as a weapon, the buckler's metal boss can deliver quick, punishing strikes. Popular with duelists and skirmishers who value mobility. This category includes targe shields and other small defensive implements designed for active parrying and striking.

### Club
**Cost:** 5 gp | **Weight:** 3 lbs | **Damage:** 4 | **Properties:** [Versatile]
**Damage Type:** [Bludgeoning]

A simple yet effective weapon consisting of a heavy, weighted end on a shorter handle. Clubs can be carved from a single piece of hardwood or feature a stone or metal head. This category includes cudgels, truncheons, shillelaghs, and similar basic bludgeoning implements. Their simplicity makes them easy to craft and wield, while their effectiveness should not be underestimated.

### Dagger
**Cost:** 15 gp | **Weight:** 1 lb | **Damage:** 4 | **Properties:** [Light], [Multi-Type], [Thrown], [Finesse]
**Damage Type:** [Piercing], [Slashing] | **Range:** 30/60 when thrown

A short-bladed weapon equally suited to stabbing, slashing, and throwing. Daggers are favored for their versatility and concealability. This category includes dirks, stilettos, poniards, parrying daggers, and similar double-edged or single-edged short blades. Some cultures favor specific styles: the rondel for armor-piercing, the misericorde for finishing blows, or the main gauche for off-hand use in dueling.

### Flail
**Cost:** 15 gp | **Weight:** 4 lbs | **Damage:** 6 | **Properties:** [Standard]
**Damage Type:** [Bludgeoning]

A hinged weapon consisting of a weighted head attached to a handle by a chain or rope. The flexible connection makes the flail particularly effective against shields and can wrap around defensive guards. This category includes various one-handed flails, such as the peasant flail (agricultural origins) and military flails with spiked or studded heads.

### Greataxe
**Cost:** 40 gp | **Weight:** 12 lbs | **Damage:** 10 | **Properties:** [Two-Handed]

A massive two-handed axe designed for sweeping, devastating blows. The heavy blade requires strength to wield but delivers tremendous cutting power. This category includes executioner's axes, broad axes, and similar oversized single- or double-bladed axes. Often associated with barbarians and berserkers, the greataxe trades finesse for raw destructive capability.

### Greatsword

A large two-handed sword with an extended blade and handle designed for powerful sweeping cuts. The pommel and crossguard can be used for bludgeoning strikes (the "murder stroke"). This category includes zweihänders, claymores, montantes, and similar large battlefield swords. Often wielded by elite infantry, these weapons require significant training but offer exceptional reach and power.

A small, light axe designed for one-handed use and throwing. Equally effective in melee or at range, making it a versatile choice for skirmishers.
