# Encumbrance & Carrying Capacity

A character's Strength score governs how much they can carry before it begins
to slow them down. Encumbrance is checked at the end of any travel period, when
suiting up in armor, or when the GM determines load is relevant. It is not
tracked round-by-round in combat.

## How Gear Is Carried

The way an item is carried determines its effective weight toward encumbrance.
Properly supported load is easier to bear than dead weight in the hands.

### Equipped (50% weight)
Worn or carried items that are properly fitted and distributed across the body:
armor laced over an arming coat, a weapon on a balanced belt and scabbard, a
shield slung on the back. These count at half their listed weight (round 
to the nearest pound).

Equipped items include worn armor, a shield (whether held or slung on the back),
and weapons in scabbards or hung on the body. 

### Frame containers (50% weight, up to container capacity)
Backpacks and rucksacks use rigid frames and shoulder harnesses to transfer
load onto the hips and back rather than the hands and arms. Contents count at
half their listed weight, but only up to the container's listed capacity.
Any weight packed beyond the capacity counts at full weight as overflow.

| Container | Capacity | Max effective weight |
|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| Backpack | 30 lbs | 15 lbs |
| Rucksack | 40 lbs | 20 lbs |

The container itself (the backpack or rucksack) counts at full weight as an
equipped item on the body.

### Body-worn pouches (75% weight, up to container capacity)
Belt pouches, belt purses, and satchels hang from a belt or shoulder strap.
They reduce effective weight somewhat, but do not distribute load as well as
a frame pack. Contents count at 75% of listed weight (round to nearest pound),
up to the container's capacity.

| Container | Capacity | Max effective weight |
|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| Belt pouch | 5 lbs | ~4 lbs |
| Belt purse | 3 lbs | ~2 lbs |
| Satchel | 15 lbs | ~11 lbs |

### Saddlebags (mount bears the weight)
Contents of saddlebags do not count toward the rider's encumbrance while
mounted. If the character dismounts and carries the saddlebags by hand, their
contents count at full weight.

### Hand-carried and unpacked (full weight)
Everything else carried without proper support counts at full listed weight. Looted armor, spare
weapons carried loose, and any items that do not fit into a container fall into this category.

## Encumbrance Thresholds

All thresholds are based on your current Strength score. Add up all effective
weights from the categories above to determine your total effective carried weight.

| Tier | Effective weight | Effect |
|------|-----------------|--------|
| Unencumbered | ≤ Str × 10 lbs | No penalty |
| **Encumbered** | ≤ Str × 15 lbs | See below |
| **Heavily Encumbered** | ≤ Str × 20 lbs | See below |
| Over maximum | > Str × 20 lbs | Cannot move |

A character cannot move while carrying more than Str × 20 lbs effective weight.
They may drop items as a [Free] action to reduce their load immediately.

| Str | Unencumbered | Encumbered | Heavily enc. | Max |
|-----|-------------|------------|--------------|-----|
| 2 | ≤ 20 lbs | 21–30 | 31–40 | 40 |
| 3 | ≤ 30 lbs | 31–45 | 46–60 | 60 |
| 4 | ≤ 40 lbs | 41–60 | 61–80 | 80 |
| 5 | ≤ 50 lbs | 51–75 | 76–100 | 100 |
| 6 | ≤ 60 lbs | 61–90 | 91–120 | 120 |
| 7 | ≤ 70 lbs | 71–105 | 106–140 | 140 |
| 8 | ≤ 80 lbs | 81–120 | 121–160 | 160 |

## Lifting and Dragging

Short-burst physical feats, distinct from sustained carry loads.

**Lifting overhead:** Up to Str × 8 lbs freely. An Might check (Hard task)
is required to lift anything above that up to a maximum of Str × 16 lbs.

**Dragging or pushing:** Up to Str × 24 lbs across flat ground. Halved on
difficult terrain. Costs 1 [Move] action per 5 feet moved.

**Powerful Build** (Nephilim, Giants, and creatures with this trait): Count as
one size category larger for all carrying, pushing, and dragging limits. All
encumbrance thresholds increase by 50%.
