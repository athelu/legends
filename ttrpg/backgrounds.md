# D8 TTRPG - Part 9: Backgrounds

## Background System

Each character chooses one background during character creation. Backgrounds represent your life before adventuring and provide:
- Skill Training: A set of skills at rank 3 or rank 4, reflecting training above the rank 2 baseline all characters start with
- Starting Equipment: Basic tools and gear for your profession
- Background Feature: A special ability or social benefit (primarily roleplay-focused)

### Background Patterns

All backgrounds provide trained skills distributed in one of four patterns, plus additional XP for customization. Rank 2 is the baseline all characters begin with; backgrounds raise specific skills above this. XP formula: 8 × current rank per step (rank 2→3 = 16 XP; rank 2→4 = 40 XP total). All patterns total 120 XP:
- Pattern A (Generalist): 5 skills at rank 3, +40 XP to spend | Skill value: 80 XP
- Pattern B (Focused): 1 skill at rank 4 + 2 skills at rank 3, +48 XP to spend | Skill value: 72 XP
- Pattern C (Flexible): 4 skills at rank 3, +56 XP to spend | Skill value: 64 XP
- Pattern D (Specialist): 1 skill at rank 4 + 1 skill at rank 3, +64 XP to spend | Skill value: 56 XP

#### Acolyte
You spent your early days in a religious monastery or cloister. You may have traveled out into the world to spread the message of your faith or because you cast away the teachings of your religion, but deep down you'll always carry within you the lessons you learned. Whether those lessons bring you comfort or haunt you probably depends on the day.
**Skills:** Religion 4, Empathy 3, History 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Iron Holy symbol, prayer book, Robe, incense stick x 10, common clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Shelter of the Faithful: You can receive free healing and care at temples of your faith.

#### Archaeologist
Crumbling ruins called to you louder than the comforts of civilization. You've spent years piecing together fragments of lost empires, and you know that the greatest treasures aren't gold, they're answers. Adventuring might have started as just another dig, but you've discovered that sometimes the most interesting artifacts are still being used by their current owners.
**Skills:** History 4, Investigate 3, Wilderness 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** journal, map case, traveler's clothes, work boots
**Feature** - Historical Insight: When examining ruins or artifacts, the character will know one useful historical fact or context clue.

#### Artisan
As an apprentice, you practiced a particular form of building or crafting, developing specialized skill. Your guild connections remain strong which is useful when you need work, materials, or a favor.
**Skills:** Craft: (choice) 3, Society 3, Persuasion 3, Devices 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Artisan's tools (choose type), common clothes, belt pouch, shoes
**Feature** - Guild Membership: You have connections with artisan guilds. Can find work, get fair prices on materials, and access guild resources in cities.

#### Artist
Your art is your greatest passion, whatever form it takes. You may adventure for inspiration, or maybe you're just trying to survive until the world recognizes your genius. You have discovered that wealthy patrons are more interested in artists who have exciting stories to tell and nothing makes for a better story than a brush with death.
**Skills:** Perform 4, Perception 3, Empathy 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Artist's tools, fine clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Artistic Reputation: Your work is recognized in certain circles. Can earn money through commissions and gain access to wealthy patrons.

#### Bandit
You took what you needed from those who had more than enough, or at least that's what you told yourself. As a member of a gang or working alone, the outlaw life taught you skills that polite society condemns but secretly envies. You might be seeking redemption, or you might just be looking for richer targets. Perhaps it's a bit of both.
**Skills:** Stealth 3, Intimidate 3, Wilderness 3, Melee Combat 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Hemp rope, common clothes, low boots
**Feature** - Outlaw Network: You know how to find and contact criminal elements in the wilderness and small settlements.

#### Barber
You have three specialties: cutting hair, treating minor wounds, and listening to gossip. People talk freely in your chair, and you've heard everything from marital troubles to murder plots. A good barber knows which secrets to keep, which to share, and when a customer really just needs someone to listen. Your steady hands and sharp razors serve you well in any situation.
**Skills:** Medicine 3, Society 3, Persuasion 3, Devices 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Razor, scissors, comb, soap, common clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Gossip Network: People talk freely during grooming. You hear rumors and local news before most others.

#### Barkeep
You poured drinks and kept the peace in an establishment where ale flowed as freely as stories. Every regular had a tale, every stranger brought news from afar, and you were right in the middle of it all. You know how to read people and make them comfortable and those are useful skills anywhere.
**Skills:** Empathy 3, Persuasion 3, Society 3, Perception 3, Intimidate 3, +40 XP
**Equipment:** Apron, common clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Tavern Talk: You know how to listen and learn and have a chance to pick up useful information in drinking establishments.

#### Barrister
Piles of legal manuals, stern teachers, and experience in the courtroom have instructed you in legal matters. You're capable of mounting a prosecution or defense in court, and you tend to keep abreast of local laws. You never know when you might need to talk your way out of trouble on short notice. Or talk your friends out of it, for that matter.
**Skills:** Society 4, Persuasion 3, Investigate 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Fine clothes, quill, ink, vellum parchment x5, shoes
**Feature** - Legal Knowledge: You understand laws and courts. Can navigate legal systems and provide counsel (though not always successfully).

#### Bookkeeper
Numbers don't lie, but the people who record them often do. You spent your days tracking accounts and managing ledgers, developing an eye for discrepancies that most people miss. Adventure is certainly more exciting than balance sheets, though sometimes the pay isn't as reliable.
**Skills:** Society 3, Investigate 3, Writing 3, Perception 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Journal, ink, quill, abacus, fine clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Head for Numbers: You can quickly assess financial records and spot discrepancies or patterns in accounts.

#### Bowyer
You shaped the tools that put arrows into the air. Everything from the bend of the stave to the tension of the string passed through your hands, and you developed an eye for what holds under pressure and what fails at the worst possible moment. 
**Skills:** Craft: Bowyer 4, Ranged Combat 3, Perception 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Work clothes, work gloves, work boots
**Feature** - Read the Weapon: When examining a ranged weapon, you immediately identify any flaws, modifications, or exceptional qualities it has. You can assess whether ammunition is properly made for a given bow.

#### Brewer
Fermentation is patient work. Knowing how to adjust variables by smell and taste until the result was worth drinking is a skill. Taverns, temples, and noble households all had use for your product, and moving between them taught you more about how communities actually function than any formal education would have.
**Skills:** Craft: Brewer 3, Society 3, Empathy 3, Persuasion 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Common clothes, work apron, shoes
**Feature** - Known in the Taproom: Brewers move between establishments as suppliers and peers rather than customers. You can find lodging and a meal at any inn or tavern for free or at steep discount by offering your professional expertise or a sample of your work. Barkeeps and tavern owners treat you as a colleague and share local information more freely than they would with strangers.

#### Butcher
Your apprenticeship involved sharp knives, practical anatomy, and an understanding of how to get the best cuts. The work was bloody, demanding, and surprisingly educational. You know more about sinew and bone than most people ever will.
**Skills:** Might 3, Medicine 3, Craft: Cook 3, Society 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Cleaver, apron, meat hook, work clothes, work boots
**Feature** - Anatomy Knowledge: You know the best cuts and methods for processing animals.

#### Carpenter
Every beam tells a story about stress and weight, every joint reveals the skill of its maker. You shaped wood into structures that will outlast you, learning patience and precision in equal measure. Adventuring might represent a departure from your careful craft, but the ability to assess a structure's integrity has saved your life more than once. Wood isn't the only thing that breaks in predictable ways.
**Skills:** Craft: Woodworker 3, Might 3, Devices 3, Perception 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Carpenter's tools, work clothes, work boots, work gloves
**Feature** - Structural Assessment: You can quickly evaluate the integrity of wooden structures and identify construction methods.

#### Cartographer
You spent years penning charts and routes for explorers, merchants, military commanders, and nobles who wanted to know what they owned. You have been to enough places that most people find your knowledge useful. You have also learned that the gaps on a map are often more informative than what fills them.
**Skills:** Craft: Cartographer 4, Wilderness 3, Perception 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Map case, journal, quill, ink, traveler's clothes, low boots
**Feature** - Surveyor's Eye: When you enter a new region or settlement, you can make an Intelligence check to recall or reconstruct useful geographic details such as patrol routes, choke points, water sources, and likely shelter.

#### Charlatan
You traveled from place to place, peddling false fortunes in one town, pretending to be a long-lost heir in the next. Becoming an adventurer might be your next big scam or an attempt to put your talents to use for a greater cause. You realize that after pretending to be a hero for so long, you've become the mask. Or maybe you're just running from that last con that went sideways.
**Skills:** Deception 3, Persuasion 3, Perform 3, Thievery 3, Empathy 3, +40 XP
**Equipment:** Fine clothes, disguise kit, playing cards, shoes
**Feature** - False Identity: You have created a second identity with documentation and can switch between identities convincingly.

#### Cobbler
You made shoes for people who never thought about shoes until they needed them. The work was steady and unglamorous. A person's footwear says something about where they've been and where they're going, and after years of repairing and rebuilding, you read that language fluently.
**Skills:** Craft: Cobbler 3, Society 3, Empathy 3, Investigate 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Sewing kit, common clothes, low boots
**Feature** - Read the Road: You can examine footwear and determine meaningful information about its owner; things like their profession, approximate wealth, and recent travel conditions. When you examine tracks or worn shoes as part of an Investigate check, subtract 1 from one die result.

#### Cook
You transformed ingredients into something that warmed both body and spirit. Whether you worked in a noble's kitchen with exotic spices or a roadside inn with whatever was available, you understand that a good meal creates community. Adventuring might pay better than kitchen work, but you still find yourself feeding your companions.
**Skills:** Craft: Cook 3, Wilderness 3, Perception 3, Society 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Apron, work clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Comfort Food: Meals you prepare during rests grant one additional HP recovery (once per rest, applies to whole party).

#### Courtier
To the common folk, court life seems like an endless parade of balls and banquets. You know better. Every smile hides an agenda, every compliment is calculated, and one misstep can mean social death, or sometimes the regular kind. You learned to navigate these treacherous waters with grace, though whether you're fleeing court intrigue or pursuing it in a new arena is anyone's guess.
**Skills:** Persuasion 3, Society 3, Empathy 3, Perform 3, History 3, +40 XP
**Equipment:** Fine clothes, signet ring, perfume, shoes
**Feature** - Court Connections: You know how to navigate noble society. Can gain audience with important figures and understand court politics.

#### Courier
Time was always your enemy. You raced against weather, bandits, and deadlines, carrying messages and packages that others deemed too valuable to trust to slower methods. The pay was decent, the life was exciting, and you saw more of the world than most people dream of. Adventuring is just a logical extension and now at least you can fight back when someone tries to rob you.
**Skills:** Athletics 3, Society 3, Perception 3, Wilderness 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Traveler's clothes, map case, riding boots
**Feature** - Swift Delivery: You know the fastest routes between settlements and can navigate unfamiliar cities quickly.

#### Criminal
As an unscrupulous independent or as a member of an underworld organization, you lived a life of crime. You might have become an adventurer to seek redemption, to escape the law, or simply to get access to bigger and better loot. Your old contacts remain useful, though maintaining those relationships means you're never quite free of your past.
**Skills:** Stealth 3, Thievery 3, Deception 3, Intimidate 3, Perception 3, +40 XP
**Equipment:** Crowbar, common clothes, belt pouch, wool hood, low boots
**Feature** - Criminal Contact: You have a reliable contact in the criminal underworld who can provide information or fence stolen goods.

#### Debt Collector
You worked for moneylenders, merchants, and noble houses, finding people who owed money and extracting it through whatever combination of persuasion and implied consequence the situation called for. The work required reading people quickly, knowing when to press and when to wait, and maintaining a professional demeanor while engaged in something most people found unpleasant. You were good at it. Whether that reflects well on you is a question you've stopped asking.
**Skills:** Intimidate 3, Empathy 3, Society 3, Perception 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Common clothes, manacles, low boots
**Feature** - Collector's Reputation: Your profession carries a weight that precedes you. Moneylenders, merchants, and civic officials recognize the role and take your stated expectations seriously.

#### Driver
Whether hauling cargo for merchants or transporting passengers, you developed a reputation for getting through when others turned back. The work was steady but monotonous, and eventually, you decided that if you were going to risk your life on dangerous roads anyway, you might as well do it for more interesting reasons.
**Skills:** Wilderness 3, Athletics 3, Society 3, Perception 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Whip, traveler's clothes, bedroll, work gloves, work boots
**Feature** - Beast Handler: You have expertise with draft animals and vehicles.

#### Dyer
Color is trade language. A specific shade of blue marks a guild, a red signals rank, a particular combination of colors identifies a house. You learned to produce those colors reliably from plant, mineral, and animal sources, which meant you also learned what those colors meant to the people who wore them. You moved through workshops and markets and occasionally noble dye-houses, always present at the margins of more consequential conversations.
**Skills:** Craft: Tailor 3, Society 3, Wilderness 3, Perception 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Common clothes, work apron, work gloves, shoes
**Feature** - Color Codes: You read guild liveries, house colors, and rank markers the way others read words. In a crowd, at court, or along a road, you can identify guild membership, noble house affiliation, military rank, and regional origin from clothing and banners. This same knowledge grants you easy entry into dye-houses, textile workshops, and guild supply chains as a recognized trade supplier.

#### Entertainer
You might have been an actor, a dancer, a musician, a street magician, or any other sort of performer. Adventuring offers new audiences and new material and every narrow escape is another dramatic moment for future performances.
**Skills:** Perform 3, Persuasion 3, Acrobatics 3, Perception 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Basic musical instrument, common clothes, disguise kit, shoes
**Feature** - By Popular Demand: You can find performance venues and earn money entertaining in most settlements.

#### Farmer
With a strong back and an understanding of seasonal cycles, you tilled the land and tended crops. Your farm could have been razed by invaders, you could have lost the family tying you to the land, or you might have simply tired of the drudgery. Whatever the reason, you still find yourself reading the weather and judging soil quality out of habit.
**Skills:** Wilderness 3, Might 3, Athletics 3, Craft: Cook 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Common clothes, work gloves, work boots
**Feature** - Rural Knowledge: You understand seasons, weather, and crops, gaining useful insights about agricultural communities.

#### Fisher
The water was your livelihood and your teacher. You might have left because the catches grew thin, or perhaps you realized the biggest fish weren't in the water at all. Either way, patience is a virtue you've mastered, and the sea's lessons apply to more than just fishing.
**Skills:** Wilderness 3, Athletics 3, Perception 3, Craft: Cook 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Net, common clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Water Sense: You understand water, tides, and aquatic life.

#### Fletcher
Crafting projectiles is precise work, and the margins for error were small enough that carelessness had consequences you could trace back to yourself. You understand projectiles in a physical, technical sense that most archers never bother to develop, and that understanding transfers to situations where precision matters.
**Skills:** Craft: Fletcher 3, Ranged Combat 3, Perception 3, Wilderness 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Quiver, common clothes, work gloves, low boots
**Feature** - Field Fletching: You can produce functional arrows or bolts from natural materials during a short rest, requiring no tools beyond a knife and access to wood and feathers. The ammunition is serviceable but not sellable. You are also more likely to recover reusable ammunition after a battle.

#### Gambler
The thrill of the win drew you into games of chance, though you know that in the long run, the house always wins, that is unless you cheat, and you definitely know how to do that. Adventuring could be a way out of debt or buy-in to the next big game. At least when you gamble with your life, the odds are more honest.
**Skills:** Deception 3, Empathy 3, Perception 3, Society 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Dice set, playing cards, common clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Never Tell the Odds: You have a knack for reading people during games of chance and can often spot loaded dice or marked cards.

#### Glassblower
Glassblowing is one of the few crafts that cannot be paused. That particular pressure shaped how you think, and a tendency to act decisively when others are still deliberating has followed you out of the workshop.
**Skills:** Craft: Glassblower 4, Arcane 3, Perception 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Common clothes, work gloves, work boots
**Feature** - Patron Contacts: Fine glasswork serves scholars, alchemists, and wealthy collectors who have few reliable suppliers. You have cultivated relationships with these patrons over years of commissioned work. In most cities you can find a scholar, alchemist, or collector who knows your name or your reputation and will offer access to their resources, libraries, or professional networks in exchange for your expertise.

#### Guard
You served in the guard, standing watch while others slept. You know the boredom of endless patrols punctuated by moments of sudden terror. Whether you left because of corruption in the ranks, disagreements with your commander, or simple wanderlust, you still think like a guard.
**Skills:** Melee Combat 3, Perception 3, Intimidate 3, Society 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Spear or club, uniform, manacles, work gloves, work boots
**Feature** - Watchful Eye: You're trained to spot trouble and know typical guard procedures in most settlements.

#### Herbalist
As a formally trained apothecary or a rural practitioner of folk medicine, you learned the healing properties of various herbs. Adventuring gives you access to rare specimens you could never afford before.
**Skills:** Medicine 4, Wilderness 3, Empathy 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Herbalism kit, mortar and pestle, common clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Plant Lore: You can identify useful plants and create basic remedies.

#### Hermit
You lived a life of solitude. Adventuring might represent your first foray out among other people in some time. This might be a welcome reprieve from solitude or an unwanted change, but in either case, you're likely still rough around the edges.
**Skills:** Wilderness 4, Medicine 3, Religion 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Robe, herbalism kit, shoes
**Feature** - Solitary Discovery: During your isolation, you discovered something significant (work with GM). People seek your wisdom, though they may not understand it.

#### Hunter
You stalked and took down animals and other creatures of the wild. The skills transfer remarkably well regardless if you're tracking a deer or a bandit, the principles are the same. You just have to hope the prey doesn't shoot back.
**Skills:** Wilderness 3, Ranged Combat 3, Stealth 3, Perception 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Bow or crossbow, arrows/bolts, high boots, work clothes, work gloves
**Feature** - Tracker: You can track creatures through wilderness and identify animal signs.

#### Investigator
You made your living uncovering truths that others wanted hidden. You developed a reputation for finding answers. Your skills at noticing details and following leads are invaluable, even if your companions find your constant questions irritating.
**Skills:** Investigate 4, Perception 3, Empathy 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Magnifying glass, journal, ink, quill, fine clothes, shoes
**Feature** - Keen Mind: When examining a crime scene or mystery, you may uncover one crucial clue or detail others might miss.

#### Jeweler
The jewelry trade sits at the intersection of craft, finance, and social currency, and you navigated all three with enough success to survive in a profession where trust is the primary material.
**Skills:** Craft: Jeweler 3, Society 3, Persuasion 3, Perception 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Fine clothes, magnifying glass, shoes
**Feature** - Appraiser's Eye: You can accurately assess the value of gems, jewelry, and precious metalwork at a glance. Merchants cannot easily deceive you on the worth of such items.

#### Laborer
You've spent years performing arduous physical labor. It was a difficult life, but you somehow survived. You may have embraced adventuring as an easier method to make your way in the world, or you might adventure under someone else's command. At least now when you're risking your life there's treasure involved instead of just a silver a day and aching muscles.
**Skills:** Might 3, Athletics 3, Devices 3, Craft: Mason 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Work clothes, work gloves, work boots
**Feature** - Hard Worker: You can find manual labor work in any settlement and are trusted by working folk.

#### Mason
You worked with stone in every way that matters: quarrying, shaping, fitting, mortaring. All meant to build things meant to outlast everyone involved in making them. The work was physical and geometrically demanding, and your eye for structural integrity developed from necessity rather than theory.
**Skills:** Craft: Mason 4, Might 3, Perception 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Common clothes, work gloves, work boots
**Feature** - Stonereader: You can assess the construction and condition of stone structures at a glance. In underground environments, you can identify worked stone versus natural formation, and estimate how recently a passage was cut.

#### Merchant
In a dusty shop or market stall you bartered wares for coin and trade goods. The skills you picked up still apply in the adventuring life, where a good deal on a suit of armor could prevent your death. You understand that everything has a price and knowing how to negotiate can be as important as knowing how to fight.
**Skills:** Persuasion 3, Society 3, Empathy 3, Investigate 3, History 3, +40 XP
**Equipment:** Fine clothes, journal, quill, ink, shoes
**Feature** - Trade Network: You have contacts in merchant guilds and can find buyers or sellers for most goods.

#### Mercenary
Bringing in lawbreakers or fighting in petty wars lined your pockets. Maybe you had an altruistic motive and genuinely believed in your employers' causes, or maybe the coin was motivation enough. Your techniques for staying alive in combat transfer easily to adventuring, though at least now you can choose your battles. Most of the time, anyway.
**Skills:** Melee Combat 3, Athletics 3, Intimidate 3, Wilderness 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Weapon of choice, common clothes, work boots
**Feature** - Soldier of Fortune: You can find mercenary work and may be recognized by other sellswords. Know how to negotiate contracts and identify legitimate employers.

#### Midwife
You were there at the beginning of more lives than you can count, and also at some endings. The work required technical knowledge, steady hands, and the ability to remain calm when the people around you were not. Families trusted you completely during the most vulnerable hours they experienced, which meant you carried information about them that shaped how they behaved toward you long afterward.
**Skills:** Medicine 4, Empathy 3, Society 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Herbalism kit, common clothes, low boots
**Feature** - Trusted in Every Home: You have stood in households during their most private and frightening hours, and that trust does not expire. Common folk in rural settlements and small towns extend you a deference and openness that strangers rarely receive. You can find shelter, honest information, and practical assistance from ordinary families who would turn away adventurers on instinct but not someone who has helped bring lives into the world.

#### Miner
You earned a living wrenching precious minerals from the lightless depths of the earth. Adventuring might have seemed lucrative or glamorous compared to this backbreaking labor and if you have to head back underground, this time you plan to do so armed with a real weapon instead of a miner's pick. At least now the things trying to kill you might be guarding treasure instead of just being cave-ins.
**Skills:** Might 3, Athletics 3, Perception 3, Craft: Mason 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Lantern, work clothes, hemp rope, work boots
**Feature** - Underground Sense: You can navigate underground spaces and identify minerals and stone quality.

#### Monk
A monastery gave you years of structured contemplation, physical discipline, and access to texts that most people never see. You were trained to think clearly, act deliberately, and maintain equanimity under conditions designed to test it. Whether you left the order willingly or were expelled, the habits it built in you are not things you can simply set aside.
**Skills:** Religion 3, Athletics 3, History 3, Medicine 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Robe, journal, shoes
**Feature** - Open Cloister: Monastic orders recognize one another's discipline regardless of doctrine. Temples, abbeys, and scriptoriums of faiths other than your own will extend you hospitality, library access, and respectful discourse when you approach as a fellow student of contemplative practice. Clergy and religious scholars treat you as a peer rather than a stranger.

#### Noble
To the common folk, the life of a noble seems one of idyllic luxury, but growing up as a noble or member of the aspiring gentry, you know the reality: a noble's lot is obligation and intrigue. Whether you seek to escape your duties by adventuring or to better your station, you have traded silks and pageantry for an adventurer's life. The question is whether your family sees this as honorable service or scandalous rebellion.
**Skills:** Society 4, Persuasion 3, History 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** Fine clothes, signet ring, shoes
**Feature** - Position of Privilege: You are of noble birth. Common folk defer to you, and you can secure audiences with authorities, though this may also make you a target.

#### Nomad
Traveling far and wide, you picked up basic tactics for surviving on the road and in unknown lands, getting by with few supplies and even fewer comforts. As an adventurer, you travel still, often into even more dangerous places. The difference is that now you have companions and you're not sure yet whether that makes things easier or more complicated.
**Skills:** Wilderness 3, Athletics 3, Perception 3, Society 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Tent, traveler's clothes, hemp rope, wineskin, work boots
**Feature** - Wanderer's Knowledge: You know routes and shortcuts across wilderness and can find nomadic communities for trade or information.

#### Pilgrim
You walked. Through Coudassian cities, across Bellicosian plains, through the mountain passes of Echartesh — following temple routes, seeking shrines, completing vows that made sense when you made them. Along the way you learned that people are more similar across national borders than governments find convenient, and that hospitality from strangers is real but conditional. You returned changed. Whether you found what you were looking for is harder to say.
**Skills:** Wilderness 3, Religion 3, Persuasion 3, Athletics 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Traveler's clothes, bedroll, waterskin, holy symbol (wood), low boots
**Feature** - Road Fellowship: Ancient pilgrim routes maintain traditions of mutual aid among travelers. You know the customs, courtesy signals, and hospitality codes that pilgrims use to identify and assist one another. Communities along established pilgrimage roads will offer shelter, food, and local knowledge to those who travel in good faith, and you know how to present yourself as exactly that.

#### Rat Catcher
Cities generate vermin, and someone has to manage the problem. You worked the warrens beneath markets and warehouses, the cellars of inns, the spaces inside walls that nobody else thought about. It was filthy, essential work that kept you in close contact with the parts of urban life that other people preferred not to acknowledge. You know how cities actually function — their hidden geography, their overlooked populations, their invisible labor.
**Skills:** Stealth 3, Perception 3, Athletics 3, Wilderness 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Common clothes, net, low boots
**Feature** - Invisible City: You mapped the forgotten infrastructure beneath the streets: cellar networks, drainage channels, service passages, and the spaces inside walls that no one else thinks about. You can navigate the hidden architecture of urban environments and enter buildings through routes that no guard has thought to watch. The city's overlooked workforce — night laborers, maintenance workers, porters, and sewermen — recognizes you as one of their own and will share information freely.

#### Refugee
You fled your homeland due to war, disaster, or persecution. Everything you knew was torn away, but somehow you survived. You could be searching for a new home, or maybe you're trying to earn enough to reclaim what was lost. The experience taught you that survival requires both strength and flexibility, and you learned never to take safety for granted.
**Skills:** Stealth 3, Wilderness 3, Empathy 3, Society 3, +56 XP
**Equipment:** Common clothes, bedroll, shoes
**Feature** - Survivor's Instinct: You know how to avoid danger and find help. Can identify safe havens and sympathetic people in unfamiliar places.

#### Sage
You have an insatiable desire for learning and sequestered yourself from the outside world to learn all you could. You read about so many wondrous places and things in your books, and always dreamed about one day seeing the real things. Eventually, that curiosity led you to leave your studies and become an adventurer.
**Skills:** Arcane 4, History 3, Writing 3, +48 XP
**Equipment:** General topic book, quill, ink, papyrus parchment x5, robe, shoes
**Feature** - Researcher: You know how to find information in libraries and archives, and have contacts among scholars who can provide access or assistance.

#### Sailor
You heard the call of the sea from a young age. Maybe you signed onto a merchant's vessel, joined the navy, or even fell in with a crew of pirates and scalawags. Life at sea taught you to work as part of a crew and stay steady when solid ground is just a memory. You might be on land now, but the ocean never really lets go of those who've sailed her waters.

#### Scholar
You pursued formal education at a university or academy, studying alongside peers and engaging in scholarly debate. Your education was broad rather than deep, covering philosophy, natural sciences, mathematics, and the arts. You've attended lectures, defended theses, and networked with the academic elite.
