# Time — Internal Manifestation

## Overview

Time internally is the manipulation of the character's own relationship with the flow of time. Not time travel, not affecting others' perception of time — that is external territory. Internal Time is the character experiencing time differently than the world around them, and the body operating at a different rate than physics normally permits.

Time primary is the subtlest and most dangerous primary energy internally. Subtle because its tells are behavioral rather than physical — a Time primary user does not glow or crack or radiate cold, they simply move and respond in ways that accumulate into wrongness over the duration. Dangerous because the body was not designed to operate outside its temporal relationship with the world, and pushing Time hard enough produces consequences that are qualitatively different from other primary energies. Exhaustion from Time primary feels different — not fatigue but disorientation, a sense of being slightly misaligned with the present moment even after the effect ends.

Time primary has the strongest initiative and reaction time effects in the system, significant craft and precision utility outside combat, and the most disorienting Presence effects. Its weakness is direct damage — Time alone does not hit hard. Time primary characters win by acting sooner, acting more often, and ensuring the fight is over before the opponent catches up.

**The line between internal and external Time:** Time primary internally affects the character's own temporal experience. Effects that slow or stop time for others, age others deliberately, or manipulate others' temporal state are external manifestation territory. The one exception is Time primary Presence effects, which by definition affect nearby creatures — but these should feel like the character's temporal field bleeding outward rather than deliberate external manipulation.

## Pairings

---

### Time / Fire
**Category:** Enhancement
**Table:** Buff/Debuff — initiative and escalating action speed
**Expression:** Combustion that develops in stages over time — Time carrying Fire produces sustained escalating intensity. The character does not spike in speed immediately. They build. Each round they maintain the effect their temporal relationship with combat accelerates slightly further. By the third or fourth round they are operating at a pace that feels genuinely superhuman.

This is the inverse of Fire/Time. Fire/Time is a long burn that does not degrade — steady intensity sustained. Time/Fire is an escalating burn — slow start, frightening finish. The escalating bonus resets if the effect ends and is reactivated. The character must sustain the effect to accumulate the full benefit.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | +1 to Initiative, at the start of each subsequent round add +1 (maximum +4 total) | 2 — 1 minute |
| 5 (4+1) | +2 to Initiative, escalating +1 each round (maximum +6 total), subtract 1 from one attack die per round | 3 — 10 minutes |
| 7 (6+1) | +2 to Initiative, escalating +1 each round (maximum +8 total), subtract 1 from both attack dice per round, one additional Minor action per round | 4 — 1 hour |

**Exhaustion:** The 7 cost tier triggers an Exhaustion check regardless of total energy spent that scene. Operating at maximum temporal acceleration across a full scene pushes the body beyond sustainable limits.
**Tell:** Movement becomes progressively faster and more precise as the duration continues. By peak acceleration the character's movements have a strobing quality — they cover distance faster than observers can smoothly track. Breath rate does not increase proportionally to movement speed, which is deeply wrong to witness.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Combat primarily. Escalating precision has minor non-combat application for tasks requiring speed accumulation — certain craft techniques, musical performance building to a climax.

### Time / Air
**Category:** Enhancement
**Table:** Buff/Debuff — subjective time and reaction windows
**Expression:** The subjective pace of time altered through Air — the character's perception of time slows relative to events around them. They are not faster — the world is slower from their perspective. The distinction is subtle but mechanically real: this affects perception and reaction, not raw physical speed. This is the most straightforward Time primary pairing and the one most characters will access first.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | +2 Agility for Reflex saves and initiative only, cannot be Surprised | 1 — 1 round |
| 5 (4+1) | +4 Agility for Reflex saves and initiative, cannot be Surprised, one additional Reaction per round | 2 — 1 minute |
| 7 (6+1) | As above + subtract 1 from both dice on the first attack made against the character each round as they perceive it coming and adjust | 3 — 10 minutes |

**Tell:** The character becomes very still between actions — their perception of the gaps between events is extended and they use that stillness. Blinks become slower and more deliberate. The character sometimes begins responding to something before it has fully happened.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. The Surprised immunity has strong non-combat utility for ambush detection and high-stakes social situations where the character needs to process rapidly shifting information.

### Time / Earth
**Category:** Adaptation
**Table:** Transformation table — temporal compression of physical processes
**Expression:** Petrification and geological force compressed into moments — Time grounded through Earth produces the body's ability to compress physical processes that normally take geological time. The character can read the temporal history of objects and places through physical contact, and can temporarily calcify their own body as a defensive measure.

The history reading is one of the most significant non-combat utility effects in the Time primary set. A character with Time/Earth active can touch a wall and perceive impressions of what has occurred in that location across time — not perfect clarity, but emotional resonance and significant events.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | Read temporal history of touched object or surface — impressions of significant events within the last month | 0 — instantaneous |
| 5 (4+1) | History reading extends to one year + temporary calcification: +3 DR all damage types for duration, movement reduced 5 feet | 2 — 1 minute |
| 7 (6+1) | History reading extends to decades + calcification: +5 DR all damage types, movement reduced 10 feet, immune to Bleeding and Grievously Wounded for duration | 3 — 10 minutes |

**Exhaustion:** The 7 cost tier triggers an Exhaustion check regardless of total energy spent. Full calcification across a scene is the body operating at geological timescales while remaining alive.
**Tell:** Skin develops a faint stone-like quality during calcification — not as dramatic as Earth/Time full petrification but visible. During history reading the character becomes completely still and unresponsive for the duration — aware but entirely focused inward.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. History reading is one of the strongest investigation and exploration utility effects in the system. Calcification is combat-focused.

### Time / Water
**Category:** Resilience
**Table:** Buff/Debuff — erosion resistance and temporal endurance
**Expression:** Erosion running in the character's favor — Time carried through Water produces the body's resistance to being worn down. Not hardness, not DR in the conventional sense, but the body's ability to persist through sustained damage that would break a less temporally grounded character. The escalating damage reduction mirrors Time/Fire's escalating bonus — both reward sustained commitment to the effect. A character who activates Time/Water at the start of a long fight and maintains it throughout is significantly more durable at the end than one who activates it midway.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | Reduce all incoming damage by 1 (minimum 1), this reduction increases by 1 every three rounds to a maximum of 3 | 2 — 1 minute |
| 5 (4+1) | Reduce all incoming damage by 2 (minimum 1), increasing to maximum 4 over the scene, +1 to all saving throws | 3 — 10 minutes |
| 7 (6+1) | As above (maximum reduction 5), +2 to all saving throws, at the end of each round regain 1 HP if the character took damage this round | 4 — 1 hour |

**Tell:** The character seems to take hits without quite accumulating the cost. Minor wounds appear briefly and then seem to recede slightly. Not healing — the damage is there, but it arrives more slowly than it should. Observers find this subtly wrong in a way they often cannot articulate.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. The escalating reduction has non-combat utility for sustained environmental exposure — extreme weather, toxin-laden environments, situations where damage accumulates slowly over time rather than in combat bursts.

### Time / Positive
**Category:** Resilience
**Table:** Buff/Debuff — accelerated healing and temporal restoration
**Expression:** Accelerated recovery that Time directs and Positive sustains — the character's body heals faster than the present moment should allow because they are pulling healing from a slightly compressed future. More controlled than Positive/Time because Time is primary — the acceleration is measured and deliberate rather than urgent. Less immediate burst, more sustained tempo.

At 5+ cost active during a long rest, the character recovers as if they had rested for 12 hours instead of 8 — full HP recovery plus removal of one additional Exhaustion level. This represents the character compressing recovery time through their temporal field.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | Regain 1 HP at start of each round, conditions affecting the character have their duration reduced by 1 round at the start of each of the character's turns | 2 — 1 minute |
| 5 (4+1) | Regain 2 HP at start of each round, condition durations reduce by 2 per turn, remove one Exhaustion level at the start of the scene | 3 — 10 minutes |
| 7 (6+1) | Regain 3 HP at start of each round, condition durations reduce by 2 per turn, remove one Exhaustion level at scene start, once per scene immediately end one condition affecting the character regardless of its remaining duration | 4 — 1 hour |

**Tell:** Minor wounds close visibly but unhurriedly — not the dramatic closing of Positive/Earth but a steady patient recovery. The character's bruising fades in the wrong order. Witnesses who watch carefully notice that the character's injuries seem to retreat rather than heal — the damage undoing itself rather than being repaired.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. Strong sustained combat recovery. One of the better long rest enhancement effects in the system.

### Time / Negative
**Category:** Expression with Presence at higher investment
**Table:** Expression sub-table for combat. Presence uses internal roll as save target.
**Expression:** Aging as damage — Time carrying Negative energy produces accelerated decay at the point of impact, forcing biological deterioration in moments. The distinction from Negative/Time: Negative/Time focuses on the entropic quality and reduces Agility as tissue ages at the surface. Time/Negative focuses on the temporal compression aspect and reduces Constitution as the body is forced to metabolize decades of wear at its core.

Constitution reduction follows the Attribute Drain rules from the core system. If Constitution is reduced to 0 the character receives the Dead condition. Reductions restore after a long rest.

**DR:** Energy (Negative), half DR.

| Primary Cost | Combat Effect | Presence (7+ only) | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | +2 Negative energy damage, target's Constitution reduced by 1 until end of scene — cosmetic aging at impact | — | 0 — instantaneous |
| 5 (4+1) | +4 Negative energy damage, target's Constitution reduced by 1 until end of scene, Weakened condition | — | 1 — 1 round |
| 7 (6+1) | +4 Negative energy damage, target's Constitution reduced by 2 until end of scene, Weakened condition | Temporal aging aura 10 feet: Fortitude save vs internal roll or Constitution reduced by 1 until end of scene | 2 — 1 minute |

**HP Cost option:** Pay 4 HP to upgrade one tier. At 7 cost with HP payment, Presence activates regardless of Mastery threshold.
**Exhaustion:** The 7 cost tier and any HP cost activation always trigger Exhaustion check.
**Tell:** Targets show localized rapid aging at impact — more pronounced around joints and core than at the surface. The character's own hands appear older than they are during activation. A faint smell of dust and old stone.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. Constitution reduction has significant impact in extended engagements — a target whose Constitution has been reduced multiple times approaches dangerous territory quickly. The visual aging effect carries weight in social encounters and interrogation contexts.

### Time / Space
**Category:** Adaptation — extreme
**Table:** No standard table applies fully. GM adjudication required for full effects.
**Expression:** Time/Space internally is the mirror of Space/Time — where Space/Time modifies the character's spatial existence to include temporal perception, Time/Space modifies the character's temporal existence to include spatial flexibility. The character does not experience space and time as fixed constraints. Their body occupies its present moment loosely.

At lower investment this produces extraordinary positional awareness. At higher investment the character begins to exist slightly displaced from the present moment in ways that make them genuinely difficult to engage.

At 7 cost, when the character would be reduced to 0 HP, they instead displace 1 round into the future — they vanish completely and reappear at the start of their next turn in the nearest unoccupied space with the damage applied but alive. This is not healing. The character is returning from a moment before the damage fully registered in their timeline. The damage still happened. They simply experienced it from a different temporal position. This is firm at once per scene.

**Mastery requirement:** Time Mastery 4+ and Space Mastery 4+ required to attempt at any cost level. Attempting below those thresholds triggers an automatic Exhaustion check at difficulty 2 regardless of outcome.

**Exhaustion difficulty:** The 5 and 7 cost tiers trigger Exhaustion checks at difficulty 2 — two successes required rather than one to avoid Exhaustion gain.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | Perfect positional awareness — always know the exact location of every creature and object within 60 feet regardless of visibility, concealment, or illusion | 2 — 1 minute |
| 5 (4+1) | As above + the character exists 1 second displaced from the present — attacks against them have Misfortune as attackers reach for where the character was rather than where they are | 3 — 10 minutes |
| 7 (6+1) | As above + once per scene when the character would be reduced to 0 HP, they displace 1 round into the future — vanish completely and reappear at the start of their next turn in the nearest unoccupied space, damage applied but alive | 4 — 1 hour |

**Tell:** The character seems to occupy space with a slight lag — their position as perceived is always fractionally behind their actual position. At 7 cost when the displacement triggers, they do not fall unconscious. They simply cease to be present. No body. No indication of where they went. They return as if stepping out of nothing.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. Perfect positional awareness is one of the strongest non-combat perception effects in the system — the character cannot be deceived about location, cannot be flanked, and perceives hidden things by their position rather than their visibility.
