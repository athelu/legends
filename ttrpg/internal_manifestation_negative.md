# Negative — Internal Manifestation

## Overview

Negative internally is entropy and decay directed through the self. This is the most dangerous primary energy to channel internally — not because it is evil, but because the body is made of the same matter that Negative energy breaks down. A character who channels Negative internally is using their own tissue as a conduit for a force that erodes tissue.

This creates the defining characteristic of Negative primary: meaningful HP costs alongside energy costs, more frequently than any other primary. These are not punishments for using the energy — they are the honest mechanical expression of what channeling entropy through a living body actually means.

Negative primary has the strongest Presence effects in the system, the best offensive debuffing, and the most significant social and psychological impact. A character built around Negative internal manifestation is feared, effective in attrition warfare, and carries visible marks of their practice. Its weakness is self-sustainability — Negative primary users burn themselves as fuel and need to manage that carefully.

**Negative energy is not necromancy.** Negative energy is entropy and decay — a natural force present in all living systems. Channeling it internally produces decay, draining, and corruption effects that work on living matter. Necromancy is the direct manipulation of pneuma — binding souls, reanimating the dead, commanding the spirit after death. That knowledge was mastered by the First Men and destroyed them. It is not available through internal manifestation or external weaving as a purchasable effect. A character who moves toward it does so through story events with GM adjudication, not through energy investment. The effects in this document are entropy. They are not necromancy.

## Pairings

---

### Negative / Fire
**Category:** Expression
**Table:** Expression sub-table
**Expression:** Life consumed as fuel for entropy — strikes carry both fire damage and Negative energy damage, consuming the target's vitality as heat. The character pays an HP cost to activate because they are literally burning their own life to power the effect. The Negative energy is primary, meaning the draining quality dominates over the heat. Targets feel the drain first and the burn second.

The HP cost buys a tier upgrade: at 3 cost with HP payment, performs as 5 cost. At 5 with HP payment, performs as 7 cost.

At 7 cost with HP payment and Negative Mastery 4+, Presence activates: creatures within 15 feet make a Will save against the internal roll result or gain the Frightened condition (Level 1 Fear).

**DR:** Both components are energy damage. Fire component: half DR. Negative component: half DR.

| Primary Cost | Bonus Fire Damage | Bonus Negative Damage | HP Cost Option | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|------------------|----------------------|----------------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | +1 fire | +1 Negative | 4 HP: performs as 5 cost | 0 — instantaneous |
| 5 (4+1) | +2 fire | +2 Negative | 4 HP: performs as 7 cost | 1 — 1 round |
| 7 (6+1) | +4 fire | +4 Negative | 4 HP: Presence activates | 2 — 1 minute |

**Exhaustion:** Always triggers Exhaustion check. HP cost plus energy cost represents significant self-expenditure.
**Tell:** Eyes darken at the edges. Heat emanates but the character is cold to the touch. Nearby flames burn lower. Targets struck feel an absence rather than an impact.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Combat primarily. Deliberate self-sacrifice framing gives non-combat narrative weight.

### Negative / Air
**Category:** Presence
**Table:** Presence — internal roll as save target
**Expression:** The suffocation of life force — Negative energy carried on Air radiates outward as a draining absence that pulls vitality from nearby creatures. Silence of the spirit rather than silence of sound. This is a pure Presence effect with no Enhancement or Expression component.

Unlike Air/Negative which drains passively in all directions, Negative/Air is directed — the character chooses which creatures within range are affected each round rather than radiating equally. This requires active focus and is why Negative is primary.

Creatures within range make a Fortitude save against the internal roll result each round. The character chooses which creatures within range are targeted each round.

| Primary Cost | Range | Effect on Failed Save | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|-------|----------------------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | 15 feet | Weakened condition, save each round | 1 — 1 round |
| 5 (4+1) | 20 feet | Weakened + Fatigued (1 Exhaustion level), save each round | 2 — 1 minute |
| 7 (6+1) | 30 feet | Weakened + Exhausted (2 Exhaustion levels), save each round | 3 — 10 minutes |

**Exhaustion:** Always triggers Exhaustion check regardless of energy spent.
**Tell:** The character's own breath becomes visible as cold vapor despite ambient temperature. Color drains from their immediate surroundings. Targets feel cold even in warm environments.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. Non-combat use — sustained directed presence in a space can weaken or drive out occupants without visible magical action. Effective for psychological pressure, area denial, and situations where visible weaving would be dangerous.

### Negative / Earth
**Category:** Expression with Adaptation
**Table:** Expression sub-table for combat. Transformation table for material interaction.
**Expression:** Rot in matter — Negative energy grounded through Earth produces decay the character can deliver through strikes or direct contact. In combat, strikes cause structural deterioration in the target. Outside combat, the character can age and decay physical materials deliberately and with precision — without leaving obvious evidence of magical tampering.

**DR:** Bludgeoning component: Physical, full DR. The decay effect is not damage and not subject to DR.

| Primary Cost | Combat Effect | Non-Combat Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------------|------------------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | +2 bludgeoning, target's DR reduced by 1 until end of scene | Age and weaken one small object — reduce structural integrity significantly | 1 — 1 round |
| 5 (4+1) | +4 bludgeoning, target's DR reduced by 2 until end of scene, Weakened condition | Age and weaken one medium object or structural element — render it unreliable or near failure | 2 — 1 minute |
| 7 (6+1) | +4 bludgeoning, target's DR reduced by 3 until end of scene, Weakened + Grievously Wounded on margin 3 | Age and collapse one large structural element — a door, section of wall, or support beam | 3 — 10 minutes |

**Tell:** Surfaces touched by the character show immediate aging — tarnishing metal, crumbling stone edges, desiccating wood. The character's own hands appear older than they are during activation.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. One of the strongest non-combat Negative pairings. Material aging has significant utility for covert entry, structural sabotage, and appraising object history.

### Negative / Water
**Category:** Expression with Adaptation
**Table:** Expression sub-table for combat. Transformation table for poison generation.
**Expression:** Slow corruption working through a living system — Negative energy carried by Water produces biological toxicity delivered through strikes. Slower than Negative/Fire but more insidious — the corruption works through the target's systems over time. The character is immune to their own biological output while this is active.

**DR:** Negative energy damage component: half DR. Poison delivery is a condition, not damage, and not subject to DR.

| Primary Cost | Combat Effect | Condition on Margin 2+ | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | +2 Negative energy damage | Weak poison (Fortitude save vs internal roll) | 1 — 1 round |
| 5 (4+1) | +4 Negative energy damage | Strong poison (Fortitude save vs internal roll) | 2 — 1 minute |
| 7 (6+1) | +4 Negative energy damage | Deadly poison (Fortitude save vs internal roll), Sickened on any hit | 3 — 10 minutes |

**HP Cost option:** Pay 4 HP to upgrade one tier on the poison severity delivered.
**Tell:** Discoloration spreads from fingertips during activation. Touch leaves a brief dark mark on skin. A chemical quality to the character's presence that others find instinctively unpleasant.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. Poison generation outside combat — introducing toxins through physical contact, contaminating substances — is significant non-combat utility.

### Negative / Positive
**Category:** Unstable — GM adjudicates
**Table:** No standard table applies. Outcome determined by relative investment and Mastery in both energies.
**Expression:** The two most fundamentally opposed energies channeled simultaneously through the body. This pairing exists at the absolute edge of what internal manifestation can do and should not be attempted without significant Mastery in both energies.

**Balanced investment** (within 1 point of each other): The energies destabilize. The character makes a Fortitude save at difficulty 2 or gains one Exhaustion level immediately. Effect is unpredictable — GM determines outcome, which may be beneficial, harmful, or both.

**Negative dominates** (Negative investment significantly exceeds Positive): Amplified Negative effect — Negative/Earth or Negative/Air effect at one tier above normal investment. The Fortitude save still applies.

**Positive dominates** (Positive investment significantly exceeds Negative): Character takes HP damage equal to the Negative investment spent. Gains a Positive effect determined by the GM — the Positive energy won but the Negative cost was paid in tissue.

**Exhaustion:** Always triggers Exhaustion check regardless of outcome or investment.
**Tell:** Light and shadow simultaneously. Wounds opening and closing. A quality of wrongness that observers find deeply disturbing regardless of outcome.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Situational. Not recommended for deliberate use.

### Negative / Space
**Category:** Presence
**Table:** Presence — internal roll as save target. No DR applies.
**Expression:** Entropy projected through spatial void — Negative energy carried by Space reaches past physical matter to touch the vitality of targets directly. This is the only Negative primary effect that bypasses DR entirely, consistent with the external pairing table rule that Negative/Space reaches what steel cannot stop.

This is entropy reaching living vitality, not manipulation of the pneuma. The target's life force is diminished by decay, not bound or commanded. The distinction from necromancy is absolute — the pneuma is weakened, not touched.

Unlike Negative/Air which radiates passively, Negative/Space is projected at specific targets. More precise, more threatening to individuals, more costly.

Targets make a Fortitude save against the internal roll result. On failure, they lose maximum HP until long rest — identical to the Life Drain mechanic in the core system.

**DR:** None. The effect reaches past physical matter entirely.

| Primary Cost | Range | Effect on Failed Save | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|-------|----------------------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | 30 feet | Target loses 2 maximum HP until long rest | 0 — instantaneous |
| 5 (4+1) | 30 feet | Target loses 4 maximum HP until long rest, Weakened condition | 1 — 1 round |
| 7 (6+1) | 30 feet | Target loses 6 maximum HP until long rest, Weakened + Frightened (Will save vs internal roll) | 2 — 1 minute |

**HP Cost option:** Pay 4 HP to upgrade one tier on the life drain effect.
**Exhaustion:** Always triggers Exhaustion check regardless of energy spent.
**Tell:** A visible distortion between the character and the target — not light bending but an absence of something. Targets describe the sensation as being briefly hollow. Animals flee the area immediately.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Combat primarily. Maximum HP reduction has pre-combat applications — weakening a target before an engagement in ways that cannot be healed away until rest.

### Negative / Time
**Category:** Expression with Presence at higher investment
**Table:** Expression sub-table for combat. Presence uses internal roll as save target.
**Expression:** Accelerated aging — Negative energy directed through Time forces decay to run at unnatural speed. Strikes carry compressed entropy — tissue and material deteriorating at the point of impact in moments rather than years. At higher investment this extends outward as an aging aura.

The visual aging effect on targets has significant social and psychological impact outside combat. A target who has been visibly aged at one point in a meeting carries that into every subsequent interaction.

**DR:** Negative energy damage: half DR.

| Primary Cost | Combat Effect | Presence (7+ only) | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | +2 Negative energy damage, target ages visibly at impact point — cosmetic only | — | 0 — instantaneous |
| 5 (4+1) | +4 Negative energy damage, Weakened condition as tissue ages at impact | — | 1 — 1 round |
| 7 (6+1) | +4 Negative energy damage, Weakened, reduce target's Agility by 1 until end of scene | Aging aura 10 feet: Fortitude save vs internal roll or Weakened | 2 — 1 minute |

**HP Cost option:** Pay 4 HP to upgrade one tier. At 7 cost with HP payment, Presence activates regardless of Mastery threshold.
**Exhaustion:** The 7 cost tier and any HP cost activation always triggers Exhaustion check.
**Tell:** The character's own hair shows temporary streaking during activation. Targets struck show localized aging at impact — graying skin, brittled surfaces, deepened lines — that fades slowly over hours. Not permanent but deeply unsettling to witnesses.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. Visual aging impact on targets carries significant weight in social encounters and interrogation contexts.
