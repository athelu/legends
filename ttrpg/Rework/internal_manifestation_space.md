# Space — Internal Manifestation

## Overview

Space internally is the most conceptually unusual primary in the system. Externally, Space governs portals, teleportation, pocket dimensions, and barriers — the manipulation of distance and location. Internally, Space cannot do those things without crossing into external manifestation territory. The boundary holds: internal Space modifies the character's relationship with their own physical reality, not their relationship with places.

This means Space primary internally is transformation territory — strange, body-horror adjacent, and deeply unsettling to observers. The character who channels Space internally does not move through space differently, they exist within it differently. Geometry that should not work. A body that violates physical expectation. Senses that perceive spatial relationships no living thing should be able to process.

Space primary has the strangest visual tells of any primary energy, significant sensory and perception utility, and the most psychologically disturbing Presence effects in the system. Characters heavily invested in Space internal manifestation develop a permanent subtle wrongness that persists even when no effect is active — repeated modification of the body's spatial relationship leaves traces.

**The line between internal and external Space:** Any Space primary internal effect that begins to resemble teleportation of others, portal creation, or movement of external objects through space crosses into external manifestation. Short-range personal displacement is internal — the body moving through its own spatial relationship. Creating a portal for another creature to pass through is external. The line is the self.

## Pairings

---

### Space / Fire
**Category:** Expression
**Table:** Expression sub-table
**Expression:** Fire placed through a spatial fold in the body's immediate field — the character's strikes deliver combustion at the exact point of contact without travel, appearing instantaneously at the impact site. This is not the body being on fire — it is the character's spatial relationship with fire being compressed to zero distance at the moment of striking.

The effect is sudden, precise, and deeply startling. Targets describe it as fire appearing from nowhere inside their guard.

**DR:** Energy (Fire), half DR.

| Primary Cost | Bonus Fire Damage | Condition | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|------------------|-----------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | +2 fire | Singed on margin 2+ | 0 — instantaneous |
| 5 (4+1) | +4 fire | Smoldering on margin 2+ | 1 — 1 round |
| 7 (6+1) | +8 fire | Ignited on margin 2+ | 2 — 1 minute |

**Tell:** No visible fire on the character. Fire appears at the point of contact only with no origin trail. The wrongness of this — flame with no source — is more disturbing to witnesses than conventional fire expression.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Combat primarily. The instantaneous placement quality has minor utility for igniting specific targets at precise points without collateral fire spread.

### Space / Air
**Category:** Enhancement
**Table:** Buff/Debuff — movement and positioning
**Expression:** Short-range personal displacement that generates kinetic force at the destination. The character does not move through the intervening space — they cease to be in one location and exist in another, and the spatial compression of that arrival impacts whatever is there.

This is personal displacement only. No other creature or object moves with the character unless being carried within normal carrying capacity.

**DR:** Bludgeoning at arrival: Physical, full DR.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | Teleport up to 10 feet to an unoccupied space, arrival deals 2 bludgeoning to adjacent creatures (Reflex save vs internal roll negates) | 0 — instantaneous |
| 5 (4+1) | Teleport up to 20 feet, arrival deals 4 bludgeoning to adjacent creatures, departure does not provoke opportunity attacks | 0 — instantaneous |
| 7 (6+1) | Teleport up to 30 feet, arrival deals 6 bludgeoning to all creatures within 5 feet, departure does not provoke opportunity attacks, can teleport to occupied space if occupying creature fails Reflex save vs internal roll (displaced to nearest unoccupied space) | 1 — 1 round |

**Tell:** The character vanishes and reappears. No blur, no trail, no indication of the path taken — simply absent, then present. The wrongness is in the absence of transition.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Combat primarily. Repositioning utility for escaping grapples, reaching elevated positions, passing through physical barriers.

### Space / Earth
**Category:** Resilience with Adaptation
**Table:** Buff/Debuff for anchoring effects. Transformation table for spatial sense.
**Expression:** Space grounded through Earth produces absolute spatial anchoring and direct perception of the structural geometry of the environment. The character becomes a fixed point in space while simultaneously developing awareness of the spatial structure around them — walls, concealed spaces, the geometry of the area perceived directly.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | Spatial anchor — immune to teleportation and planar displacement against will, structural sense of immediate area 30 feet | 2 — 1 minute |
| 5 (4+1) | As above + immune to all forced movement physical or magical, structural sense 60 feet including concealed spaces | 3 — 10 minutes |
| 7 (6+1) | As above + extend spatial anchor to willing creatures within 5 feet, sense exact dimensions and composition of all structures within 60 feet | 4 — 1 hour |

**Tell:** The character seems slightly more present than normal — more solid, more placed. Other people instinctively avoid walking through their space. A faint resistance felt by anyone attempting to physically move them even casually.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. Structural spatial sense is one of the strongest non-combat utility expressions in the Space primary set — invaluable for dungeon navigation, finding concealed doors and passages, evaluating structural integrity, and detecting hidden spaces.

### Space / Water
**Category:** Adaptation
**Table:** Transformation table — fluid spatial existence
**Expression:** Space channeled through Water produces the character's ability to exist fluidly within their spatial field, moving through tight spaces, around obstacles, and past barriers that should restrict a solid body. The character does not become intangible — they become spatially flexible, their body finding the path through space that water would find.

Observers who see a character pass through a wall make a Will save (difficulty 1) or gain the Disoriented condition briefly.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | Move through spaces as small as 6 inches without the Squeeze penalty, no movement cost for difficult terrain | 2 — 1 minute |
| 5 (4+1) | As above + move through solid barriers up to 6 inches thick (walls, doors) as if difficult terrain, no opportunity attacks while moving | 3 — 10 minutes |
| 7 (6+1) | As above + barriers up to 2 feet thick, immune to Grappled and Restrained conditions, cannot be held by physical restraints | 4 — 1 hour |

**Tell:** The character moves through tight spaces without apparent effort. Crowds part around them in ways that seem off. The body finds angles that should not fit. Deeply wrong to observe at close range.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. Infiltration, escape from restraints, navigation of confined spaces — the strongest non-combat movement utility in the Space primary set.

### Space / Positive
**Category:** Resilience
**Table:** Buff/Debuff — spatial protection and deflection
**Expression:** Positive energy anchored spatially creates a field immediately around the character that redirects harmful effects. Not armor, not toughness — the space the character occupies becomes slightly resistant to intrusion by damaging forces. The spatial field pushes back against what enters it.

At 7 cost, the field can redirect incoming ranged attacks to a different target within 15 feet. This requires no action — the spatial field simply routes the incoming force elsewhere. The character chooses the new target when the trigger occurs. The new target makes a Reflex save against the internal roll result.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | +2 DR against ranged attacks and ranged weaves only | 2 — 1 minute |
| 5 (4+1) | +2 DR all damage types, ranged attacks against the character have Misfortune | 3 — 10 minutes |
| 7 (6+1) | +4 DR all damage types, ranged attacks have Misfortune, once per round redirect one ranged attack to a different target within 15 feet | 4 — 1 hour |

**Tell:** Incoming projectiles seem to deflect slightly — attacks that should strike flush glance off at odd angles. The air around the character has a faint resistance quality. Not visible but felt by anyone who reaches toward them.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. The protective field has non-combat utility in environments with physical projectile hazards — collapsing structures, rockfalls, wind-driven debris.

### Space / Negative
**Category:** Presence
**Table:** Presence — internal roll as save target. No DR applies.
**Expression:** The character's spatial field becomes an absence. Not darkness, not cold, but a wrongness in the space they occupy that reaches past physical matter to disturb the pneuma of nearby creatures. Space carrying Negative energy creates an eldritch presence that bypasses physical resistance entirely — the effect is not in the world, it is in the gap between the world and what lies beyond.

This is the most psychologically disturbing Presence effect in the system. Creatures do not fear this the way they fear a burning Presence — they experience a wrongness they cannot name, a conviction that the space the character occupies should not exist.

Creatures within range make a Will save against the internal roll result each round.

**DR:** None. The effect reaches past physical matter. Consistent with the external pairing table rule for Space/Negative.

| Primary Cost | Range | Effect on Failed Save | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|-------|----------------------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | 10 feet | Disoriented condition, save each round | 1 — 1 round |
| 5 (4+1) | 15 feet | Disoriented + Frightened (Level 1 Fear), save each round | 2 — 1 minute |
| 7 (6+1) | 20 feet | Disoriented + Fleeing (Level 2 Fear), save each round | 3 — 10 minutes |

**Exhaustion:** Always triggers Exhaustion check regardless of energy spent.
**Tell:** The character's outline seems slightly uncertain — not blurred, but as if the eye cannot fully commit to where they end and the space around them begins. Shadows near them behave incorrectly. Other creatures instinctively increase distance without knowing why.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. The wrongness aura has significant non-combat utility for clearing spaces, preventing pursuit, and psychological impact in social situations. A character maintaining Space/Negative in a negotiation room will affect the proceeding in ways that cannot easily be attributed to them.

### Space / Time
**Category:** Adaptation — extreme
**Table:** No standard table applies fully. GM adjudication required for full effects.
**Expression:** The deepest pairing in the Space primary set — Space and Time together internally represent the character's relationship with the fabric of reality itself becoming malleable. This does not produce conventional damage and does not map cleanly to standard tables. It produces the most extreme and least predictable internal manifestation effects in the system.

At lower investment the effects are powerful but bounded. At higher investment this pairing approaches the boundary of what is possible for internal manifestation and begins to strain the distinction between internal and external expression.

**Mastery requirement:** Space Mastery 4+ and Time Mastery 4+ required to attempt at any cost level. Attempting below those thresholds triggers an automatic Exhaustion check at difficulty 2 regardless of outcome.

**Exhaustion difficulty:** The 5 and 7 cost tiers trigger Exhaustion checks at difficulty 2 — two successes required rather than one to avoid Exhaustion gain.

| Primary Cost | Effect | Secondary Duration Cost |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| 3 (2+1) | Perceive 3 seconds into the future — cannot be Surprised, subtract 1 from both dice on the first attack made against the character each round | 2 — 1 minute |
| 5 (4+1) | As above + once per scene, retroactively declare that one of the character's own actions this round did not happen — the action is undone, energy spent is recovered, effects are reversed | 3 — 10 minutes |
| 7 (6+1) | As above + the character exists slightly outside normal time — take turn simultaneously with the highest initiative in the round regardless of own initiative result, once per scene only | 4 — 1 hour |

**Note on the 5 cost retroactive undoing:** This is the most powerful single effect in the internal manifestation system. It is firm at once per scene. It cannot undo another character's action — only the character's own action taken this round.
**Tell:** Deeply wrong in ways that are difficult to describe. The character seems to already know what is about to happen. They respond to things before they occur. At 7 cost, witnesses cannot fully account for the character's position — they appear to be in two moments simultaneously.
**Combat/Non-Combat:** Both. Future perception has strong non-combat utility for detecting ambush, reading the immediate future of a conversation, and anticipating natural events. The retroactive undoing has non-combat applications at GM discretion.
